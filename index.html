The three rules

Discovered in Act Two

You'll discover these when the large document fails. They work on every AI system you will ever use. They do not expire when the model changes.

1
Under 20 pages → attach the file
The model sees most of the content. It works.
2
Over 20 pages → paste the section
You control what the model sees. No truncation.
3
Always ask for the exact quote
If it cannot quote it, it invented it. Confidence is not accuracy.

The three prompts

Copy · paste · run
Prompt A Summarize
Attach your document · Works on any document under 20 pages
[System Context] You are a precise analyst. [Task] Summarize this document in five bullet points that a busy colleague understands in thirty seconds. [Constraints] Use only the attached document. Each bullet must be one complete sentence. [Output Format] Five bullet points. No introduction. No conclusion.
Prompt B Extract Obligations
Attach your document · Best for contracts, policies, directives
[System Context] You are a compliance analyst. [Task] Find every commitment, deadline, obligation, and named responsible party in this document. [Constraints] Use only the attached document. For each item, quote the exact sentence from the document. [Output Format] Numbered list. Each item: — The obligation (one sentence) — Responsible party (if named) — Deadline (if stated, or "not stated") — Exact quote from the document
Prompt C Find The Gap
Attach your document · The one that surprises people
[System Context] You are a critical reviewer. [Task] What is the single biggest gap or unresolved question this document does not address? [Constraints] Stay within the document. Name the section where the gap is most visible. [Output Format] Part 1: The gap (one sentence) Part 2: Where it appears (section or page reference) Part 3: Why it matters (two sentences)

The four-part prompt anatomy

[System Context] → who the model is and what it is working with [Task] → one verb: summarize, extract, find, compare [Constraints] → what to use, what to avoid, what to quote [Output Format] → exact structure — no ambiguity

Common errors: two verbs in the task · no output format · no constraints · generic system context. Each error removes one layer of control.

Where does this break?

The threat model

You're building an agent next year that reads internal policy documents and flags overdue obligations. Where does it fail without anyone noticing?

Truncation
The agent reads 8% of the document. Misses obligations buried on page 40. Reports nothing overdue. Everything looks fine.
Confidence without accuracy
It flags invented obligations with the same tone as real ones. You act on something that was never written.
Document identity confusion
In a batch of ten policies, it attributes an obligation from policy three to policy seven. Wrong team gets flagged.
No citation
It gives a confident answer but cannot quote the source. You cannot verify it. You cannot challenge it.

The three rules prevent all four of these failures. That is why they exist.
